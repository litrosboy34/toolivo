Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Unix timestamp?

A Unix timestamp is a numeric time format that counts the seconds or milliseconds since January 1, 1970 UTC.

Are timestamps stored in UTC?

Unix timestamps are based on UTC. This converter also displays your local date so you can compare both formats easily.

What is the difference between seconds and milliseconds?

Unix timestamps commonly use 10 digits for seconds and 13 digits for milliseconds. This tool detects both formats automatically.
