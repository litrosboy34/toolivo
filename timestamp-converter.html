What is a Unix timestamp?

A Unix timestamp is the number of seconds or milliseconds since January 1, 1970 UTC.

Seconds or milliseconds?

Online Unix Timestamp Converter

A Unix timestamp represents the number of seconds or milliseconds that have passed since January 1, 1970 UTC. Developers use timestamps for databases, APIs, logging systems and software development.

Are timestamps stored in UTC?

Unix timestamps are based on UTC. This converter also shows your local date so you can compare both formats easily.

What is the difference between seconds and milliseconds?

Unix timestamps commonly use 10 digits for seconds and 13 digits for milliseconds. This tool detects both formats automatically.
